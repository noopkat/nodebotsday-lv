Nodebots Day - Las Vegas

July 26th 2014

Nodebots Day, on 26th July 2014, is a hack day for javascript developers to get together and learn about hardware and build awesome robots with friends over pizza. You will teach yourself how to talk to arduino over serial, build things with popsicle sticks, rubber bands, electronics and have a great time!

We also have a strict code of conduct we expect all attendees to follow, ensuring everyone has an exceptionally good day.

What is a Nodebot?

NodeBots are robots that are controlled by node.js. A NodeBots event is a full day event where JS developers team up in groups of 3 with soldering irons and parts nearby to create amazing robotic devices. At a NodeBots event, teams of up to 3 people hack together Arduino based robots and program it all using just JavaScript! If you have no idea what an LED is or a resistor for that matter, have no fear - NodeBot experts will be readily available to help you hardware hack, solder, and 3D print your way to a full fledged NodeBot. At the end of the day we’ll have a Sumo Competition interlaced with project demos from anyone who wants to present. Source: nodebots.io Photos from last year's event:

Sumobot Jr? Sounds like a wrestling robot!

Almost! The Sumobot Jr is a low cost, home rolled NodeBot designed by Pawel. You can clone the repo with the cutting plans and parts list on Github. We’ll be lending out some of these kits on the day for you to assemble. Alternatively feel free to bring in your own parts and supplies to make something you can take home! The Sumobot Jr costs around $50 to make which is pretty sweet. Some of the cheapest pro ones out there start at $100.
